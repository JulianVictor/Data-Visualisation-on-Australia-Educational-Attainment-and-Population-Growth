Australia Educational Attainment And Population Growth by Julian Jee Chet Shiang and Pravin Sharavanan

Education is a crucial determinant of individual and societal progress. In Australia, educational attainment varies significantly across different states and territories. Understanding these variations and their trends over the years can provide valuable insights into the efficacy of educational policies and socioeconomic development. This project aims to analyze the educational attainment levels across Australian states by year to identify patterns, progress, and areas needing improvement. The motivation behind this project is to contribute to the ongoing efforts to enhance educational outcomes and to inform policymakers, educators, and researchers about the current state and trends in education across the country.

The primary aim of this project is to comprehensively analyze and compare the percentage of educational attainment levels across different Australian states for a specified range of age over a specified period. By doing so, the project seeks to uncover trends, disparities, and factors influencing educational outcomes, ultimately providing actionable insights for policy development and educational reforms.
